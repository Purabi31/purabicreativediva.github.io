About the Project

Joy is a free, easy-to-use home for your wedding. Manage guests, RSVPs, and more—all in one place. And most importantly, capture the joy of your special day and showcase it for generations. With Joy, you get your own beautiful website and app. It's a fun and easy way to share everything that is important about your wedding with your loved ones. Joy is a startup for weddings, backed by the Y Combinator Summer 2016 Batch. Joy builds a virtual home for weddings–a website and an app–which allows couples to manage wedding planning details in one place. Eventually, Joy's goal is to build a virtual bridesmaid that helps with every stage of wedding planning.

My Role

I was the UX researcher and illustrator/junior designer. In the beginning, I conducted and reviewed findings from our user interviews. Later on, I drew storyboards and helped finalize the redesign of Joy's mobile app through wireframes and high-fidelity mockups

Challenge

Redesign the Joy iOS mobile app to encourage guests to capture the "Joy" that takes place during weddings.


Undestanding the Audience

Everyone has a mobile phone in their pocket. In addition to standard photos and videos, what are creative things we can do to these media formats to let guests better express the joy they experience at these events? How do we encourage guests to capture and share while making it friction-less? How do we make it a fast and fluid experience? For users that don’t create content, how do they engage?

Seeking user feedback

We reached out to three different participants to research and gain user feedback for the current Joy app. We wanted to analyze users' pain points within the current Joy experience, and validate ideas that emphasize capturing joy.

Positive feedback

+ Wedding countdown
+ Concept: Planning every stage of wedding
+ & sharing moments

Pain Points

- to navigate through
- Want more customization with moments
– Want more interaction with wedding guests


Design requirements

We wanted to redesign Joy in a positive manner for each end user with an emphasis on capturing and sharing the joy of weddings. In order to get feedback on our ideas from the company mentors, we first outlined some design requirements for the Joy iOS mobile app as a whole.

  • A successful redesign will fit into the visual design language and brand already established.
  • Users should know or quickly learn that capturing moments is a primary action used in the app.
  • Users should want to capture moments and be encouraged to participate.
  • Users should feel they are creating unique and creative content to share.
  • The experience should feel social and human.
  • Keep in mind the consumption experience for users viewing the content.
  • Keep in mind how the content is packaged and viewed for years to come.

Wirefames

With the design requirements in mind, we proposed some features and changes to begin "capturing the Joy." As the team's illustrator, I drew storyboards for Joy's target users. We identified four main target users of Joy:

  • Attending guests
  • Brides & grooms
  • Guests who could not attend
  • Admins – wedding planners, photographers, maids/men of honor

Using storyboards, we wanted to envision different scenarios in which the intended users could use Joy - before, during, and after a wedding.



High Fidelity Wireframes

Home page redesign

appearance of the home page changes depending on whether it's before, during, or after a wedding, to show relevant content during that time.

Popular Moments leaderboard

Subtle leadership board to encourage users to be featured on the home page by capturing beautiful Moments during the wedding.

Capturing and customizing Moments

Giving the opportunity to customize a Moment through camera filters, overlay text, fun wedding stickers, and tagging the Moment.

Moments Page

Reorganization of the Moments page by adding filtering capabilities with search, tag filtering, and other filtering options.

Personalized message feature

Allowing users to interact with each other through personal messages, such as guests who couldn't attend. Those guests can still send their congratulations through a personal message.

E-thank you cardPersonalized message feature

Brides, grooms, and admins can send their thanks to their beloved ones with customized, personal video collage messages with a preset wedding tune


With this redesign of the Joy iOS app, we'll end with our vision for our redesign: to emphasize capturing and sharing moments that happen at weddings, and providing a fulfilling experience for all through Joy.
